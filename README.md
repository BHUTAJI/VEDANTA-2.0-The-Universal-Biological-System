### Abstract

VEDANTA 2.0 is an exploratory interdisciplinary framework proposing that the universe may be interpreted not solely as a mechanical structure, but as a dynamic, self-organizing energetic system governed by resonance, cyclic interaction, and systemic interdependence.

The framework combines concepts inspired by systems theory, nonlinear dynamics, wave organization, resonance modeling, and contemplative cosmological interpretation. It introduces the idea of a “Cosmic Respiration System,” where matter is understood as a temporary energetic stabilization process emerging through harmonic interaction and magnetic structuring.

Central to the model is the proposed “3-6-9 Harmonic Architecture,” representing symbolic phases of energetic differentiation, resonance stabilization, and structural manifestation. The framework further explores toroidal geometry, recursive energetic loops, and magnetic organization as possible conceptual mechanisms underlying matter formation and cosmic dynamics.

**VEDANTA 2.0 is presented as a conceptual and speculative systems model rather than an empirically validated scientific theory.** Its purpose is to encourage interdisciplinary discussion, computational simulation, theoretical modeling, and future empirical investigation across fields such as systems science, theoretical cosmology, consciousness studies, resonance dynamics, and complex systems research.

**The framework does not claim metaphysical certainty or established physical proof.** Instead, it serves as an exploratory bridge between contemplative inquiry and contemporary scientific abstraction.
